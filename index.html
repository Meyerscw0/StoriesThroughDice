D&D Service-Learning Project: Data, Interpretation, and Identity Correlations

Introduction

This project surveyed 15 individuals about their Dungeons & Dragons characters, motivations, and personal connections to the characters they created. The goal was to understand who participated, what they created, and why they approached character creation the way they did.

[cite_start]

A central success emerged quickly: Over half the participants were new to D&D, with 53% playing for less than a year and one-third being first-time players[cite: 1].

6. Analysis Conclusion

The data reveals that character creation served different emotional roles depending on identity and experience. Beginners displayed the strongest personal connection to their characters, while experienced players experimented more with mechanics.

7. Project Process & Reflections

The 15 player surveys were gathered from a group of players that I personally led through a guided character creation exercise. This hands-on approach was challenging but rewarding.

What Went Well

The collaborative aspect was a major success. Players highlighted the fun of designing aesthetics and workshopping ideas together.

Challenges

Scheduling was difficult, as was ensuring clarity when explaining mechanics to complete newcomers.

8. Final Conclusion

I learned a great deal about these players and their stories. Overall, this dataset shows D&D as a powerful environment for creativity and identity expression—especially for newer players discovering these tools for the first time.
